Cedarburg

Cedarburg is a small city located in Ozaukee County, Wisconsin, United States. It is located about 20 miles north of Milwaukee. It is home to rich history and popular festivals all year round.

History

Cedarburg was founded in the early 1840s by German immigrants who settled around Cedar Creek. In the late 1840s, German immigrants began making Cedarburg into a rural trading center. They built mills, a hotel, and a pharmacy. Now-in-days, this area still remains as Cedarburgâ€™s economic center.

Throughout the rest of the 19th century, the German community created two Turnhalles in Cedarburg and a neighboring area. Turnen is the German word for gymnastics. In Cedarburg, the German community used these buildings for gymnastics, theater performances, balls, and other big celebrations.

After World War II, suburbanization had a major impact on the city of Cedarburg. Cedarburg experienced quick population growth that brought growth of new commercial properties and housing developments. Although the city went under major changes, about 200 historical buildings have been preserved to this day. Cedarburg also began hosting lively festivals and events throughout the year to attract visitors from all over.

Demographics

Population: From July 1, 2022, the population of Cedarburg, WI is estimated to be 12,421.

Median Property Value: The median property value was $339,100 in 2021. Compared to the national average of $244,900, this number is 1.38 times larger.

Median Household Income: In 2021, the median household income is $79,935 in Cedarburg, WI.

Number of Households: In 2021, the number of households were about 5,120.

Work Commute Time: Employees in Cedarburg, WI have an average commute time of 23.3 minutes. Employees in Cedarburg, on average, have a shorter travel time to the normal US worker with 26.8 minutes.

Interurban Trail

Interurban Trail is a 27.2-mile point-to-point trail near Cedarburg, Wisconsin. The trail goes through rural and urban areas connecting several communities through its paved 27.2-miles.The trail is open year-round with plenty of activities to partake in, like birding, biking, in-line skating, walking, running, cross-country skiing and hiking. The trail is dog-friendly for dogs that are on leashes. The Interurban Trail is considered as easy route and takes an average of 8 hours and 19 mins to complete.
